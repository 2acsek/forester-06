COOLING(H4SO) > Radiator Fan System

INSPECTION

DETECTING CONDITION:

• Engine coolant temperature is 95°C (203°F) or more.

• Vehicle speed is 19 km/h (12 MPH) or below.

TROUBLE SYMPTOMS:

Radiator main and sub fans do not rotate under the above conditions.

STEPCHECKYESNO

1.CHECK OPERATION OF RADIATOR FAN.

1) Connect the test mode connector.

2) Turn the ignition switch to ON.

3) Using the Subaru Select Monitor, check the forced operation of the radiator fan relay.

NOTE:

• When performing a forced operation radiator fan relay check using the Subaru Select Monitor, the radiator main fan and sub fan will repeat low speed revolution → high speed revolution → OFF in this order.

• Subaru Select Monitor

Refer to Compulsory Valve Operation Check Mode for detail procedures.

Do the radiator main and sub fans rotate at low speed?

2.CHECK OPERATION OF RADIATOR FAN.

1) Connect the test mode connector.

Radiator main fan system is normal.

3.CHECK POWER SUPPLY TO MAIN FAN RELAY.

1) Turn the ignition switch to OFF.

2) Remove the main fan relay from the A/C relay holder.

3) Measure the voltage between the main fan relay terminal and chassis ground.

Connector & terminal

(F27) No. 31 (+) — Chassis ground (−):

Is the voltage 10 V or more?

Connector & terminal

5.CHECK FUSE.

1) Remove the fuse from the A/C relay holder.

2) Check the condition of fuse.

Is the fuse blown out?

Replace the fuse.

6.FUSE TERMINAL AND MAIN FAN RELAY TERMINAL HARNESS INSPECTION.

Does the main fan rotate?
