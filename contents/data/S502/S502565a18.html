AUTOMATIC TRANSMISSION(4AT) > ATF Cooler Pipe and Hose

REMOVAL

1. ALL MODELS EXCLUDING THOSE WITH ATF COOLERS (WITH WARMER FEATURE)

1. Set the vehicle on a lift.

2. Remove the battery and washer tank.

3. Lift the vehicle.

4. Remove the under cover.

5. Disconnect the ATF cooler hose from the radiator.

6. Disconnect the ATF cooler hoses from the pipes.

NOTE:

• Do not use a screwdriver or other pointed tools.

• If it is hard to remove the hose, wrap the hose with cloth to prevent from damaging it, and while turning with pliers, pull straight out by hand.

7. Disconnect the ATF cooler pipe from frame.

8. Remove the ATF cooler inlet and outlet pipes.

NOTE:

When separating the outlet pipe, do not lose the ball and spring used with retaining screw.

(A)

ATF cooler inlet pipe

(B)

ATF cooler outlet pipe

2. ALL MODELS WITH ATF COOLERS (WITH WARMER FEATURE)

1. Set the vehicle on a lift.

2. Remove the air intake chamber.

3. Disconnect the inlet and outlet hose.

(A)

ATF inlet hose

(B)

ATF outlet hose

4. Disconnect the transmission harness connector, and remove from the stay.

5. Remove the oil charge pipe.

6. Disconnect the ATF cooler assembly from the transmission, and set aside.

7. Disconnect the air breather hose.

8. Remove the oil cooler inlet and outlet pipes.

NOTE:

When separating the outlet pipe, do not lose the ball and spring used with retaining screw.

(B)
